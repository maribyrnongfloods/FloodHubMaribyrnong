Source data attributions
========================

Streamflow records
------------------
- Bureau of Meteorology, Water Data Online, http://www.bom.gov.au/waterdata/
  Gauges 230100A, 230102A, 230106A, 230107A, 230119A, 230211A, 230237A
  (Melbourne Water Corporation data; quality-controlled "Water Course Discharge",
  series DMQaQc.Merged.AsStored, sub-daily aggregated to a time-weighted daily mean).
  CC-BY-4.0.
- Victorian Government Water Measurement Information System (Hydstra),
  https://data.water.vic.gov.au/
  Gauges 230200 (Maribyrnong @ Keilor), 230202 (Jackson Ck @ Sunbury),
  230206 (Jackson Ck @ Gisborne). Daily mean discharge (variable 141.00).
  CC-BY-4.0 (Victorian Government).

Catchment polygons
------------------
- Melbourne Water Stream Network v1.3.1 (Walsh & Kunapo 2023)
  Werg group, School of Geography, University of Melbourne.
  https://tools.thewerg.unimelb.edu.au/mwstr/
  CC-BY-4.0.
  Polygons derived by tracing upstream sub-catchments via the MWSTR
  `subcs.nextds` graph, unioned per gauge, then simplified to ~110 m tolerance
  for Earth Engine compute tractability (max area drift 0.23 %). Each gauge has
  a distinct polygon; nested gauges (e.g. Konagaderra < Bulla < Keilor Nth <
  Chifley Dr) are properly contained, not duplicated.

Static basin attributes
-----------------------
- HydroATLAS / BasinATLAS (Linke et al. 2019, Sci. Data 6:283)
  https://www.hydrosheds.org/page/hydroatlas
  CC-BY-4.0.
  Derived via the Caravan_part1_Earth_Engine.ipynb pipeline.

Meteorological forcings
-----------------------
- ECMWF ERA5-Land (Muñoz Sabater 2019, doi:10.24381/cds.e2161bac)
  Copernicus Climate Change Service (C3S) Climate Data Store.
  Licensed under the Copernicus License (Creative Commons-compatible).
  Aggregated from hourly to daily in local time using the gauge coordinates,
  via the Caravan_part2_local_postprocessing.ipynb pipeline.

Independent cross-validation sources (cited under fair dealing for research)
============================================================================

- Melbourne and Metropolitan Board of Works (March 1986)
  Maribyrnong River Flood Mitigation Study, Report MMBW-D-0040.
  Catchment areas at Keilor (1,312 km²), Bulla (874 km²), Sunbury (338 km²).

- Jacobs Group Pty Ltd (March 2023)
  Maribyrnong Flood Event October 2022 — Post Event Analysis.
  Commissioned by Melbourne Water. Independent peak-flow records and
  Bayesian LP3 flood frequency analysis.

Both sources confirm the MWSTR-derived catchment areas to within ~1.5 % on
every primary gauge.
