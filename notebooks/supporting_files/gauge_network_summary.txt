1 station excluded — agency duplicate (Feb 2026):
  230104A  Jacksons Creek at Sunbury Road, Sunbury
           Co-located with Hydstra gauge 230202 (same physical gauge, ~10 m
           separation). 230104A is the Melbourne Water agency ID; 230202 is
           the Victorian Water / Hydstra ID. 230202 kept: record from 1960
           vs 1996 (36-year longer record).
           Source: Oct 2022 post-event flood analysis named it "Jacksons
           Creek at Sunbury Road" — confirming it is NOT on the mainstem.

 MAINSTEM & DEEP CREEK — Melbourne Water API (api.melbournewater.com.au)
================================================================================

1. ausvic_230100 | Deep Creek at Darraweit Guim
   Station ID  : 230100A
   Lat / Lon   : -37.4103, 144.9023
   Area        : 682.7 km²
   Flow from   : 1996-01-01
   Role        : Upper Deep Creek; dominant contributor to Maribyrnong flood
                 peaks (Oct 2022: 280 m³/s, near 1% AEP). Deep Creek is a
                 major tributary — NOT the Maribyrnong mainstem.
   Name source : Jacobs/Melbourne Water Oct 2022 post-event flood analysis.
                 Previously misnamed "Maribyrnong River at Darraweit".

2. ausvic_230102 | Deep Creek at Bulla  *** ADDED Feb 2026 ***
   Station ID  : 230102A
   Lat / Lon   : -37.6314, 144.8010
   Area        : 876.4 km² (HydroATLAS UP_AREA, polygon intersection)
   Flow from   : 2004-01-01
   Role        : Junction gauge capturing combined Deep Creek + Emu Creek
                 flows before entering the Maribyrnong mainstem. Closes the
                 water balance gap between Darraweit Guim (682.7 km²) and
                 Keilor (1305.4 km²). Oct 2022 peak: 98 m³/s.
   Source      : Identified from Oct 2022 post-event flood analysis.
                 CAMELS AUS v2 check Feb 2026: not present — safe to include.
                 MW API has flow from 2004; pre-2004 level data from 1996.

3. ausvic_230211 | Bolinda Creek at Clarkefield
   Station ID  : 230211A
   Lat / Lon   : -37.4662, 144.7440
   Area        : 177.9 km²
   Flow from   : 2008-01-01
   Role        : Upper Bolinda Creek tributary. Despite being 45% smaller than
                 Jacksons Creek, provided similar flood flow in Oct 2022 due
                 to Emu Creek arm and high antecedent wetness.
   Name source : Jacobs/Melbourne Water Oct 2022 post-event flood analysis.
                 Previously misnamed "Maribyrnong River at Clarkefield".

4. ausvic_230107 | Konagaderra Creek at Konagaderra
   Station ID  : 230107A
   Lat / Lon   : -37.5285, 144.8560
   Area        : 682.7 km²
   Flow from   : 1996-01-01
   Role        : Konagaderra Creek tributary entering mid Maribyrnong.

5. ausvic_230237 | Maribyrnong River at Keilor North  *** ADDED Feb 2026 ***
   Station ID  : 230237A
   Lat / Lon   : -37.6778, 144.8050
   Area        : 1413.8 km² (HydroATLAS — Level-12 resolution snaps Keilor North,
                 Keilor, and Chifley Drive to the same outlet basin; true catchment
                 is likely 900–1200 km² but no finer estimate is available)
   Flow from   : 2008-01-01
   Role        : Junction gauge downstream of Jacksons Creek confluence,
                 in Sydenham Park. Captures combined mainstem + Jacksons
                 Creek flows. Sits between Sunbury (406.7 km²) and Keilor
                 (1305.4 km²). Used in official MW flood warning operations.
   Source      : Station number confirmed from Maribyrnong Municipal Storm
                 and Flood Emergency Plan v4.0 (Oct 2018), Table A4.
                 CAMELS AUS v2 check Feb 2026: not present — safe to include.

6. ausvic_230106 | Maribyrnong River at Chifley Drive
   Station ID  : 230106A
   Lat / Lon   : -37.7659, 144.8950
   Area        : 1413.6 km²
   Flow from   : 1996-01-01
   Role        : Lower mainstem, tidal zone. Flow only reliable above ~1 m
                 water level (>16,520 ML/day). Only 263 valid flow days on
                 record. 3rd highest stage on record in Oct 2022 (4.22 m).
                 Highest recorded: 4.50 m (September 1906).
   AEP levels  : Minor 1.70 m (20% AEP), Major 2.90 m, 1% AEP = 4.40 m
                 (source: Maribyrnong Municipal Storm and Flood Emergency
                 Plan v4.0, Oct 2018, Appendix C1).

7. ausvic_230200 | Maribyrnong River at Keilor
   Station ID  : 230200
   Lat / Lon   : -37.727706, 144.836476
   Area        : 1305.4 km² (official Victorian Water figure)
   Flow from   : 1907-05-20
   Role        : Primary long-record mainstem gauge. Artificial crump weir;
                 586 gaugings 1908–2025. Longest record in the network.
                 Oct 2022: 768 m³/s (highest since 1974, just above 2% AEP).
   Note        : BOM also assigns this station ID 230105A (same physical gauge,
                 different agency system). We use the Hydstra/DELWP number.
